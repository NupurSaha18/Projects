Historical places of Bangladesh

Mahasthangarh

Mahasthangarh is one of the oldest archaeological sites in Bangladesh, located near Bogura city. Dating back to at least the 3rd century BCE, it was once the capital of the ancient kingdom of Pundravardhana. Visitors can see the massive fortified walls, ancient ruins, and artifacts like terracotta plaques, coins, and inscriptions that tell the story of a once-thriving city. Sixty Dome Mosque (Shat Gambuj Masjid)

Located in Bagerhat, the Sixty Dome Mosque is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the finest examples of Sultanate-era architecture in Bangladesh. Built in the 15th century by Khan Jahan Ali, it actually has 77 domes and beautiful arches supported by thick brick pillars. The mosque is renowned for its impressive architecture and historic significance, serving both as a place of worship and a community center during its time.

Lalbagh Fort

Lalbagh Fort is an incomplete Mughal fortress in the heart of Dhaka city. Construction started in 1678 under Mughal prince Muhammad Azam, son of Emperor Aurangzeb. The fort complex includes several historical structures like the tomb of Pari Bibi, the Diwan-i-Aam (audience hall), and a beautiful mosque. Lalbagh Fort is a major tourist attraction, offering a glimpse into Mughal history and architecture in Bangladesh.
